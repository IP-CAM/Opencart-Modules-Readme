After installing the module, the number of sessions in Google Analytics decreased

A drop in reported traffic in Google Analytics after implementing Consent Mode is normal and expected. Google Analytics only starts tracking a user after they have given explicit consent for the use of cookies. If a user declines or simply doesn't interact with the consent banner, their session won't be included in your analytics data.

This behavior is in line with modern privacy regulations like GDPR and ePrivacy, and ensures compliance with legal requirements.

Can I set the default consent to “granted” without user interaction?

No, and here's why:

Setting consent to "granted" by default — without user interaction — violates modern privacy regulations like GDPR (EU), ePrivacy, and similar laws in other regions (such as California's CCPA/CPRA).

These laws require that users explicitly opt in to the use of non-essential cookies (such as those used for tracking and advertising).

If you pre-grant consent without asking the user, you risk:

  • Violating legal requirements
  • Being penalized or fined by regulatory authorities
  • Losing user trust

The purpose of Consent Mode is to help you stay compliant by only activating tags once the user agrees. While this may reduce the number of tracked sessions, it protects you legally and aligns with best practices.

Google Analytics doesn't detect active consent signals

If you're not seeing active consent signals in Google Analytics immediately after implementing Consent Mode, don't worry — this is not unusual.

It can take between 24 to 72 hours for Google Analytics to start reflecting updated consent data. During this period, the system processes and verifies the consent signals coming from your website. Be sure to also verify your implementation using Google Tag Assistant to ensure everything is set up correctly.
